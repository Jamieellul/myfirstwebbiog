Getting around

The main content of this web-site consists of 20
questions concerning what we have been taught at
Code Nation [II] about IT and coding; prompting
us to reflect on how we built our site - as we
went. Answers and thoughts are grouped in 4
sections called 'Outcomes'.

Links to other Units and Outcomes,
Acknowledgments & References
and about me are at the
top of this page.

On each unit's outcome page, you can click on the answer
you wish to read at the beginning. Or else you can
scroll downthrough them. There are options to click
and return tthe top of the page after every answer.

I have used a command called ‘target_blank’ on all links to
other websites, so that they open in a new tab at the top of
the screen. This is so you can leave them open to read fully
later and click back on my page tab to resume.

Other links to personal photos and images- I’ve named
‘blip-memes,’ are not coded to open in a new tab. They
were intended to momentarily accentuate a point under
discussion, visually, for a second or so, but I had
trouble solving that (discussed in answer 1.2.4).

The exception is my ‘archivist’ photo above, which does open in a
new tab - for accessibility reasons. Because I’m hoping, if you are
not internet experienced, that you’ve seen the visible tab options
up top and, so, been able to switch back. In future, click on the
browser back button (an arrow pointing left that’s at top left of
your screen) to return from my ‘blip-memes’ to my pages.

I hope you make it to the end without keeling over...
